<p><p>Face-based access control systems are a type of biometric technology that use facial recognition technology to control access to facilities or systems. These systems use digital cameras to capture images of individuals' faces, and then compare them to a database of authorized individuals. If a match is found, the system grants access, otherwise, access is denied.</p><p>The face-based access control systems market is experiencing significant growth due to several factors. The increasing need for enhanced security and the demand for contactless solutions in various industries are driving the adoption of these systems. Additionally, advancements in facial recognition technology, such as improved accuracy and speed, are further fueling market growth.</p><p>Various industries, such as government, banking and finance, healthcare, and transportation, are adopting face-based access control systems to ensure secure access to their premises or systems. In the government sector, these systems are used to control access to sensitive areas, such as government buildings or military facilities. In the healthcare sector, they are used to secure access to medical records or restricted areas within hospitals. The banking and finance sector is also witnessing significant adoption of face-based access control systems to secure access to ATMs and banking facilities.</p><p>The COVID-19 pandemic has also contributed to the market growth of face-based access control systems. With the need for contactless solutions, these systems have gained popularity as they eliminate the need for physical contact like touching keypads or fingerprint scanners.</p><p>The face-based access control systems market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 6.7% during the forecast period.
