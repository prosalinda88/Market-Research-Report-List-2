<p><p>Military Coastal Radar is classified into two types: X-Band Radar and S-Band Radar. X-Band Radar operates at a frequency of 8 to 10 GHz and offers high resolution and accuracy. It is mainly used for short-range tracking and surveillance purposes. S-Band Radar operates at a frequency of 2 to 4 GHz and provides a longer range coverage. It is effective for detecting and tracking larger objects such as ships and aircraft. Both types are essential for military coastal surveillance, with X-Band Radar focusing on precision and S-Band Radar covering larger areas.</p></p>
