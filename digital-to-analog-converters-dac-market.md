<p><p>Digital-to-Analog Converters (DACs) are electronic devices that convert digital signals into analog signals, enabling the conversion of digital data into a continuous waveform. The market for DACs can be categorized into different types. The R-2R DAC market utilizes resistors in a network to convert digital input into analog output. String DACs use a string of resistors to produce the analog signal. Current source and sink DACs generate analog output by controlling currents. Other types of DACs include Delta-Sigma, PWM, and multiplying DACs, which employ various techniques to convert digital signals into analog form.</p></p>
