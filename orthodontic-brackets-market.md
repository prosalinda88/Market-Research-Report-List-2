<p><p>Orthodontic brackets are small metal or ceramic devices bonded to teeth in order to facilitate the movement of teeth during orthodontic treatment. They are an integral part of braces and serve as anchorage points for orthodontic wires and elastics. Brackets work in conjunction with other orthodontic appliances to correct malocclusion and provide a desirable alignment of the teeth and jaws.</p><p>The orthodontic brackets market has witnessed significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue growing at a CAGR of 6% during the forecast period. The rising prevalence of dental issues such as overcrowding, misalignment, and malocclusion across the globe is a major factor driving market growth. Additionally, the increasing demand for aesthetic orthodontic treatments is also contributing to market expansion.</p><p>Advancements in orthodontic brackets technology have fueled market growth. Manufacturers are developing braces with improved aesthetics, such as tooth-colored or clear brackets, to enhance patient comfort and acceptance. Moreover, the introduction of self-ligating brackets, which eliminate the need for elastic or metal ligatures, has gained traction due to their advantages in reducing treatment duration and discomfort.</p><p>Another trend in the orthodontic brackets market is the use of computer-aided design and computer-aided manufacturing (CAD/CAM) technology. This technology enables the fabrication of customized brackets, tailored to each patient's specific dental anatomy, resulting in better treatment outcomes and patient satisfaction.</p><p>Increased awareness about oral aesthetics and the availability of advanced orthodontic brackets in emerging economies is expected to create lucrative opportunities for market players. However, the high cost of orthodontic treatments and the availability of alternative teeth straightening methods could pose challenges to market growth.</p><p>Overall, the orthodontic brackets market is poised for steady growth due to the increasing demand for orthodontic treatments, advancements in bracket technology, and the growing focus on dental aesthetics.</p></p>
