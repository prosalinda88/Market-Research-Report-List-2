<p><p>Solid-state drives (SSDs) are storage devices that use flash memory to store data. There are different types of SSD technologies available in the market, including Single-Level Cell (SLC), Multi-Level Cell (MLC), and Triple-Level Cell (TLC). SLC technology stores one bit of data per cell, offering high performance and endurance but at a higher cost. MLC technology stores multiple bits per cell, striking a balance between performance, endurance, and cost. TLC technology, on the other hand, stores three bits per cell, providing lower cost but sacrificing some performance and endurance compared to SLC and MLC.</p></p>
