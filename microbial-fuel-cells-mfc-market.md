<p><p>Microbial Fuel Cells (MFC) are devices that use microorganisms to convert the chemical energy in organic matter into electrical energy. There are two types of MFC markets: Mediator Microbial Fuel Cell and Mediator-free Microbial Fuel Cell. In the Mediator MFC market, a mediator compound acts as an intermediate between the microorganisms and the electrode, facilitating electron transfer. Conversely, Mediator-free MFCs utilize direct electron transfer between the microorganisms and the electrode, eliminating the need for a mediator compound. Both types have their respective advantages and applications in various industries.</p></p>
