Silicon diode temperature sensors function by measuring the variation in electrical resistance with temperature. Thermocouples, on the other hand, generate a voltage proportional to the temperature difference between two dissimilar metals. Other types of cryogenic temperature sensors include resistance temperature detectors (RTDs), which utilize the change in electrical resistance of a metal wire with temperature. These sensors are essential in various applications, such as superconductivity, low-temperature physics, and cryogenic engineering.</p></p>
