<p><p>Electromagnetic Therapy Devices are categorized based on the frequency at which they operate. High-frequency devices use electromagnetic waves with a frequency of over 10^6 Hz and are often employed for cosmetic and surgical applications. They generate heat and are known for their ability to accelerate healing and tissue repair. On the other hand, low-frequency devices operate at frequencies below 10^6 Hz. They are commonly used for pain relief and muscle stimulation, as they can penetrate deep into tissues. Both types of devices offer therapeutic benefits but target different medical conditions.</p></p>
