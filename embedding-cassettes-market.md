<p><p>Embedding cassettes are used in histology laboratories to hold tissue samples during the embedding process. Glass embedding cassettes are made of glass, providing transparency and excellent chemical resistance. They are commonly used for special staining techniques. On the other hand, polymethylpentene embedding cassettes are made of a lightweight, durable plastic material that offers good heat resistance. They are preferred for routine histology applications and are less prone to breakage compared to glass cassettes. Both types serve the purpose of securely containing tissue samples during processing.</p></p>
