<p><p>Soluble fibers market includes different types such as Inulin, Oligosaccharides, Resistant Starch, Resistant Maltodextrin, Polydextrose, Beta-glucan, and others. Inulin is a naturally occurring carbohydrate found in plants and has various health benefits. Oligosaccharides are derived from natural sources and provide prebiotic properties. Resistant Starch is a type of dietary fiber that resists digestion in the small intestine. Resistant Maltodextrin is a soluble fiber that acts as a low-calorie bulking agent. Polydextrose is a synthetic soluble fiber used as a sugar replacement. Beta-glucan is a type of fiber found in oats and barley, known for its cholesterol-lowering properties. The "Others" category includes various soluble fiber types not specifically mentioned above.</p></p>
