<p><p>The electromagnetic induction furnace market includes two main types: coreless electromagnetic induction furnaces and channel electromagnetic induction furnaces. A coreless electromagnetic induction furnace operates by using a coil to create a magnetic field that induces electrical currents in the metal being melted. This type of furnace is typically used for smaller batches of metal. On the other hand, a channel electromagnetic induction furnace utilizes a channel system to guide the molten metal along a designated path. This type of furnace allows for continuous melting and is commonly used in larger-scale industrial applications.</p></p>
