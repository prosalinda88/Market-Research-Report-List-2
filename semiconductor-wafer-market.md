<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The Semiconductor Wafer Market is comprised of two types: BEOL (Back-end of Line) and FEOL (Front-end of Line). BEOL refers to the process that takes place after the transistors and other active components have been fabricated on the wafer. It involves the creation of interconnects, metal layers, and insulating materials to connect the various components. On the other hand, FEOL refers to the process of fabricating the active components themselves on the wafer, such as transistors, diodes, and resistors, using techniques like photolithography and etching.</p></p>
