<p><p>Vaccine particulate adjuvants have various types based on the administration route: oral, subcutaneous, intranasal, intramuscular, intradermal, and others. Oral adjuvants are delivered through the mouth, while subcutaneous ones are injected under the skin. Intranasal adjuvants are sprayed or dropped into the nostrils, intramuscular ones are injected into the muscle tissue, and intradermal ones are injected into the skin layers. Other types may include adjuvants administered through alternative routes, such as intravenous, intraperitoneal, or transdermal. These different administration routes provide various benefits and help to stimulate an effective immune response against targeted diseases.</p></p>
