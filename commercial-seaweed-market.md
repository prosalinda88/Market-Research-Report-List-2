<p><p>The commercial seaweed market is categorized into three major types: red, brown, and green markets. Each market represents a different type of seaweed. The red market mainly consists of species like Porphyra and Dulse, which are commonly used in sushi and as food additives. The brown market includes species like kelp and wakame, primarily utilized in the production of alginates, food products, and cosmetics. Lastly, the green market consists of species like Ulva and Sea Lettuce, which are used for human consumption and in the production of biofuels and fertilizers.</p></p>
