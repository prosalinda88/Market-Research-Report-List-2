<p><p>Vascular plugs are medical devices used in interventional radiology and cardiology procedures to occlude blood vessels. They are metallic devices typically made of Nitinol, a nickel-titanium alloy that can be easily compressed and expanded. Vascular plugs come in different sizes and shapes, with the most common being cylindrical or conical.</p><p>The vascular plugs market has witnessed significant growth in recent years and is projected to continue growing at a CAGR of 6.70% during the forecast period. This growth can be attributed to several factors. Firstly, the increasing prevalence of cardiovascular diseases worldwide has led to a rise in the number of interventional procedures, thereby driving the demand for vascular plugs. Additionally, advancements in technology have improved the effectiveness and safety of these devices, further boosting their adoption.</p><p>Moreover, vascular plugs offer several advantages over traditional embolization techniques, such as coils or liquid embolics. They provide reliable and immediate occlusion of blood vessels, reducing the risk of reperfusion or recurrence of bleeding. Vascular plugs also allow for precise positioning and can be retrievable or detachable, providing flexibility to the procedure.</p><p>In terms of market trends, there has been an increasing focus on the development of novel vascular plugs with enhanced features. For instance, companies are investing in research and development to introduce plug designs that provide better vessel wall apposition and reduce the risk of migration. The development of innovative plug designs and customization options are some of the latest trends in the market. <p><p>Vascular plugs are medical devices used in interventional radiology procedures to block or occlude blood vessels. These plugs come in different types, including Titanium-Nickel (Nitinol) and Titanium-Nitride (Tin). Nitinol vascular plugs are made of an alloy primarily composed of titanium and nickel, known for their shape memory and superelastic properties. Tin vascular plugs, on the other hand, are coated with a layer of titanium nitride, which enhances their durability and biocompatibility. Both types of vascular plugs are designed to effectively and safely occlude blood vessels, providing reliable treatment options for various medical conditions.</p></p>
