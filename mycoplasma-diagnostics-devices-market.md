<p><p>Mycoplasma diagnostics devices market includes various types of testing methods. PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction) is a technique that amplifies and detects DNA to identify mycoplasma infection. Immunoassay tests measure specific antibodies or antigens to determine if the organism is present. DNA staining involves labeling the DNA of mycoplasma to visualize its presence under a microscope. Microbial culture techniques involve growing mycoplasma in laboratory conditions to identify and study the infection. Finally, there are other methods used for mycoplasma detection, such as nucleic acid hybridization, flow cytometry, and gene sequencing.</p></p>
