<p><p>Hemodialysis and Peritoneal Dialysis are two different methods used to treat patients with kidney failure or end-stage renal disease (ESRD).</p><p>Hemodialysis is a process where a machine called a dialyzer is used to filter the blood. During a hemodialysis procedure, the patient's blood is pumped outside their body into the dialyzer, which acts as an artificial kidney, removing waste and excess fluids. The cleaned blood is then returned to the patient's body.</p><p>On the other hand, Peritoneal Dialysis involves using the peritoneum, a membrane in the abdomen, as a filter. Dialysis fluid is introduced into the peritoneal cavity through a catheter and left there for a specific amount of time. The fluid absorbs waste and toxins from the patient's blood. After the required time, the fluid is then drained from the body, carrying the waste with it.</p><p>The Hemodialysis and Peritoneal Dialysis Market have shown significant growth in recent years. This growth can be attributed to various factors such as the increasing prevalence of kidney diseases, the rising geriatric population, and advancements in dialysis technology. Moreover, the increasing awareness about the treatment options for renal diseases among patients and healthcare professionals has also contributed to market growth.</p><p>The market has witnessed several trends, including the development of portable and home-based dialysis machines, which provide convenience and flexibility to patients.
