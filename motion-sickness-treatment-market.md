<p><p>Motion sickness is a common ailment caused by the brain receiving conflicting signals from the eyes and inner ears. Treatment options for motion sickness include medications that fall into different market types. Anticholinergic drugs work by blocking the action of a neurotransmitter called acetylcholine, which helps reduce nausea and vomiting. Antihistamines, on the other hand, block the release of histamine, which is involved in allergic reactions and can also trigger nausea. Other treatment options may include natural remedies or alternative therapies, such as acupressure wristbands or ginger supplements.</p></p>
