<p><p>The food texture market consists of various types of additives that enhance the texture of food products. Thickening agents add viscosity to liquids and are commonly used in soups, sauces, and desserts. Gelling agents create a gel-like consistency and are often found in jellies, yogurt, and bakery fillings. Emulsifiers help mix ingredients that would otherwise separate, such as oil and water, commonly used in mayonnaise and salad dressings. Stabilizers maintain the consistency and prevent separation of ingredients in food products like ice cream and whipped cream. Other additives include anti-caking agents, binding agents, and bulking agents, which contribute to the overall texture and quality of food.</p></p>
