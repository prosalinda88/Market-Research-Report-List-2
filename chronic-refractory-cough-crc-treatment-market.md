<p><p>Chronic refractory cough (CRC) treatment refers to the management and alleviation of persistent cough that remains unresponsive to conventional therapies. CRC is often defined as a cough that lasts for more than eight weeks despite appropriate investigation and treatment. The condition can significantly impact an individual's quality of life and may be caused by various underlying factors such as postnasal drip, asthma, gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD), or laryngeal hypersensitivity.</p><p>Treatment strategies for CRC involve a multidimensional approach, which may include lifestyle modifications, medications targeting specific underlying causes or suppressing cough reflex, behavioral therapy, and in some cases, surgery. However, due to the complexity and heterogeneity of this condition, finding an effective treatment can be challenging, and a personalized approach is often necessary.</p><p>The chronic refractory cough (CRC) treatment market is expected to experience significant growth during the forecast period.
