<p><p>Nuclear radiation detectors are devices used to identify and measure the presence of radiation in various environments. Gas ionization detectors use a gas-filled chamber to create ions when radiation interacts with the gas. Semiconductor detectors utilize semiconducting materials that produce electrical signals in response to radiation. Scintillation detectors contain a scintillating material that emits light when exposed to radiation. Each type of detector has its own strengths and weaknesses, and they are commonly used in industries such as healthcare, nuclear power, and environmental monitoring to ensure safety and security.</p></p>
